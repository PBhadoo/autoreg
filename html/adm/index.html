How to validate a domain request

  • Choose a request in the request list;
  • Prioritary requests are flagged in green, duplicates are flagged in red;
  • Check that it seems to comply with the policy of EU.org and the policy of the relevant subdomain;
  • Choose an action on this request:
    Accept
    • Check that the WHOIS info is correct and well formatted.
      1. address: should be a correctly formatted address
      2. person: should be a correctly formatted name (John Smith and not Smith John nor John SMITH)
      3. nic-hdl: (if present) should exist (check the InterNIC or RIPE Whois database) and should point to the right person.

        Despite warnings everywhere, some people persist in inventing a NIC handle for themselves. Don't hesitate to edit these out by replacing admin-c: and tech-c: by the full name as it appears in the person: line, and delete the nic-hdl: line.

      If you detect an error, correct it and press the Submit button.
    • Modifications to the EU.org WHOIS base are automatically handled by the validation script. There should be a WHOIS entry for every domain!
    • Zone modifications are automatically validated.
    Reject
    Fill the appropriate explanation field Reason, it will be included in the rejection mail sent to the requester. Click Reject to send the mail.
    Delete quietly
    Same as Reject, except no mail is sent.
    Mark as answered
    This is used to mark the request as waiting for further information from the requester. Just click on the relevant button.
